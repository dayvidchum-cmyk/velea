# PER-STAR SUPPORTS — all 27 nakshatras · David's doctrine, 2026-07-20

*Given in answer to "should the cited nature drive every star's score, or only overrule
contradictions?" His answer was **neither**, and it reframed the question.*

---

## Why neither

> "No single group of Nakshatras is universally considered the 'best' for every purpose. The
> suitability of a day depends on the complete Muhurta — including the Nakshatra, Tithi, Vara, Yoga,
> Karana, the Moon's strength, planetary influences, **and the specific activity being undertaken**."
>
> "These seven Nakshatras are best understood **not as universally 'lucky' days**, but as reliable
> and traditionally respected foundations within the broader science of Muhurta."

So the seven are not a blanket +1. They are favourable **for particular kinds of work**, which means
a single scalar mode-score is the wrong instrument for them entirely. What matters is **what each one
supports** — and that is a list, not a number.

---

## The seven, in his words

### The three Uttaras — Dhruva (Fixed/Stable)

> Fixed stars are traditionally chosen for endeavours intended to **endure over time**: constructing
> buildings, purchasing property, establishing organizations, installing deities, entering long-term
> agreements, or beginning projects meant to have lasting impact.

**Uttara Phalguni — the Star of Patronage.** Sun-ruled; deity Aryaman — friendship, honourable
relationships, contracts, patronage, social responsibility.
*Supports:* marriage and partnerships · contracts and legal agreements · leadership responsibilities
· business relationships · long-term commitments.

**Uttara Ashadha — the Star of Lasting Victory.** Sun-ruled; the Vishwadevas. Righteousness,
perseverance, success earned through integrity rather than shortcuts.
*Supports:* beginning long-term enterprises · leadership roles · public responsibilities · important
life decisions · projects requiring endurance and discipline.

**Uttara Bhadrapada — the Star of Inner Stability.** Saturn-ruled; Ahirbudhnya. Depth, maturity,
patience, emotional steadiness — slow, sustainable growth rather than quick success.
*Supports:* spiritual practice · long-term financial planning · research · education · building
enduring foundations · deep personal transformation.

### Hasta — the Star of Skill · Kshipra (Swift)

> Moon-ruled; deity Savitur. The hand: craftsmanship, precision, communication, practical competence.
> Favourable for activities that benefit from quick execution or dexterity.

*Supports:* learning new skills · beginning business activities · artistic work · writing ·
negotiations · healing practices · crafts and technical work.

### Punarvasu — the Star of Renewal · Chara (Movable)

> Jupiter-ruled; deity Aditi. "Becoming good again" — restoration, abundance, protection, the ability
> to recover after setbacks. **Valued not because it guarantees success**, but because it supports
> resilience and second chances.

*Supports:* travel · relocation · restarting projects · education · business launches · purchasing
vehicles · recovery and renewal.

### Shravana — the Star of Learning · Chara (Movable)

> Moon-ruled; deity Vishnu. Listening, learning, preserving knowledge, establishing order.

*Supports:* studying · teaching · public speaking · seeking advice · signing educational agreements ·
organizing systems · administrative work · travel.

### Shatabhisha — the Star of Healing and Investigation · Chara (Movable)

> Rahu-ruled; deity Varuna. "The Hundred Physicians" — healing, medicine, diagnosis, restoration;
> also scientific inquiry, research, technology, uncovering hidden truths. **Because it is
> Rahu-ruled it can emphasise isolation, unconventional approaches or intense focus — powerful but
> not necessarily gentle, and suited to healing and analytical work rather than all activities
> indiscriminately.**

*Supports:* medical treatment · research · scientific work · technology · meditation ·
detoxification · investigation · problem solving.

## THE DEFECT THIS EXPOSED

The engine's day-character carries a `supports` list, and it already reaches the model and is already
woven into the prose. But those supports come from the **nature**, not the star. There are only seven
natures for twenty-seven stars, and for some stars the nature's list is simply **wrong**:

| Star | What the engine currently says the day supports | What his doctrine says |
|---|---|---|
| **Shatabhisha** | *movable* — "travel, moves and relocations, vehicles, change of direction" | healing, research, science, technology, meditation, detox, investigation |
| **Shravana** | *movable* — same travel list | studying, teaching, public speaking, seeking advice, organizing, administration (travel too) |
| **Uttara Phalguni** | *fixed* — "foundations, construction, planting, vows" | marriage, partnerships, contracts, legal agreements, leadership, patronage |
| **Uttara Bhadrapada** | *fixed* — same foundations list | spiritual practice, long-term financial planning, research, education, deep transformation |

**On a Shatabhisha day the app tells the reader it is a good day for buying a vehicle.** The star of
the Hundred Physicians, read as a moving day, because it shares a class with Swati and Dhanishtha.

This is a genuine data-accuracy defect — the same class as the karaka drift, but one level finer:
the table is not wrong, it is not *granular enough*, and the coarseness produces false statements.

## The fix, and its limit

Per-star supports for these seven, overriding the nature list where he specified one. The other
twenty stars keep the nature-level list, which is cited and correct at that grain.

**The limit, stated:** his descriptions are the source for these seven. They are recorded verbatim
above so the origin is never in doubt — this is his method statement, not a classical citation, and
it should not be mistaken for one when the remaining twenty are eventually filled in.

### The check that mattered

**His nature for every one of the 27 agrees with the cited canon** (`muhurta-tables.json`, Muhurta
Chintamani + Brihat Samhita 98). Zero disagreements, verified programmatically and pinned by a test.
So this adds specificity **on top of** the sourced table rather than overruling it — which is the
only way it was safe to take.

### One spelling trap, caught

His table writes **Dhanishta**; the canon and the engine emit **Dhanishtha**. A key that does not
match the emitted name is a silent no-op — the exact bug audit M11 found in the nakshatra modifiers,
where `'Dhanishta'` never once matched and one star quietly carried no modifier at all. Canon
spelling used, and pinned.

### His own caveat, kept

> "Different classical texts and regional traditions do not agree on every classification, especially
> for nakshatras like Shatabhisha, Krittika, and Vishakha… the central principle remains the same:
> **the nakshatra's nature should align with the purpose of the activity, and the full muhurta — not
> the nakshatra alone — determines whether a time is considered auspicious.**"

That caveat is why the day's character still runs the whole filter — tithi family, vara, vetoes,
tārā and chandra bala — and the star's supports are one input to it, not the verdict.
