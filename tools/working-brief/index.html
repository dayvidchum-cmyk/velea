Tithi as cadence — your proposal (7/22 night), not yet formalized
The Knot supplies the noun; the Tithi supplies the verb. Reframe the tithi from good/bad into the day's rhythm of action via the five families as a repeating 5-beat pulse — Nanda (nourish) · Bhadra (build) · Jaya (break through) · Rikta (release) · Purna (complete): Begin → Build → Break-through → Empty → Complete. The families ALREADY exist in the engine (day-filter.ts, auspiciousness.ts) — but only as favorability (Rikta −1 + veto, Purna +1). So this is a narrative reframe of an existing detector (the "no black & white" law: Rikta = release, not "bad"), not a new scorer. Lead the prose with the pulse, keep the 15 single-tithi temperaments as a deeper layer; the veto stays structural.

The family-turn — cost & friction checked (your question, 7/22). A tithi averages ~23.6h (synodic month 29.53d ÷ 30), just under a day, so a calendar day touches ~2 tithis on average (one boundary) — rarely 1 (a long tithi engulfs the day) or 3 (a short "kshaya" day). Never a soup. And because the five families cycle every 5, consecutive tithis are ALWAYS adjacent families — so a two-tithi day is a single step along the pulse (Bhadra→Jaya = "builds toward breaking through"), read as momentum, not a jump. Cost ≈ zero: the tithi + day-edge longitudes are already computed (Velea already picks a majority tithi); the second family is mod-5 arithmetic, no extra ephemeris; and the turn is a texture inside the one existing day read, no second LLM call. Guardrail: cap at the turn (from→to), never enumerate; a 3-tithi day uses majority + the dominant turn — one arc, one sentence (same as the star-turn).
