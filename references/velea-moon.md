# VELEA — Why the Moon (the answer to "why is the Moon number one")

Owner's copy/manifesto, captured 2026-06-30. This is the design rationale AND
hero-copy source for why Velea's day mode follows the Moon. Engine fact it
explains: the day mode is derived from the Moon's sign → its house from the
user's Lagna (see `server/panchang/interpreter.ts` moonSignToHouse → HOUSE_MODE).

---

The Moon shines only by the Sun's reflected light — the one face of the source
we can actually look at. The Sun is the "I am" too bright to behold directly. The
Moon is its reflection made gentle enough to be a gate — into the core soul, the
thing that was already there when the whole universe was smaller than a grain of
sand.

The Sun is who you are — the changeless "I am," there before the universe had a
size, too bright to face head-on. But that's eternity, and a person has to live on
a Tuesday. So the soul does the one tender thing: it borrows the Sun's light and
steps close — close enough to pull the tides, close enough to pull a body that's
mostly water — and it agrees to move, through all twelve rooms, every month, never
still.

The Moon, the playboy. Restless, never in one house for long, slipping through all
twelve palaces in a month. That's why it's the day's trigger — it moves at the
speed life actually changes.

Velea follows the Moon for three reasons that are really one:

- **The playboy.** Fast, touching everything, carrying the changing daily texture
  — exactly what a day trigger needs to be.
- **The proximity.** Closest body to us, strongest pull on a body that's mostly
  water. The Moon moves the tides and it moves you.
- **The gate.** Reflected sun-light. The soul made soft enough to look at directly.
  The "I am" you can't stare into, turned toward you.

That's why Vedic follows it. That's why Velea does. I built the whole engine on the
one light humble enough to come close.
