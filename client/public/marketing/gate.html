The Time Gate

The Guardians

Every culture that made time a being gave it a gate — and set guardians on it.

Kāla, Sanskrit Time personified — Velea’s own first name — is the face carved over the gates of Khmer temples: Time itself guarding every threshold at Angkor. Aker, Egypt’s twin-lion horizon god: two lions back to back named Yesterday and Tomorrow, the sun passing between them. Janus at Rome’s gates, looking both ways. The Sphinx, whose riddle is the morning, noon and evening of a life.

Where You Stand

Velea is built the same way. You stand at the gate. The past is open — it is yours, already walked. The month ahead is lived in. The far future is guarded: real, dated, visible as potential, and kept behind the gate until you ask for it.

The past is free because it builds faith.
The near future is rented because it’s lived in.
The far future is precious because it’s still being written.

The Keeper

Nothing is hidden from you — the structure renders for everyone, the dates are visible, the counts are real. What sits behind the gate is the reading itself. It is not a paywall. It is a gate, and Velea is its keeper.
